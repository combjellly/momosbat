WHAT'S A BAT MITZVAH?

"Bar Mitzvah" literally means "son of the commandment." "Bar" is "son" in Aramaic, which used to be the vernacular of the Jewish people. "Mitzvah" means "commandment". "Bat" means daughter. So a Bat Mitzvah is a Bar Mitzvah for a girl. Whew!

Under Jewish Law, children are not obligated to observe the commandments. At the age of 13 (12 for girls), children become obligated to observe the commandments. The Bar Mitzvah ceremony formally marks the assumption of that obligation along with the corresponding right to take part in leading religious services and to marry.

Bar Mitzvah is not about being a full adult in every sense of the word (e.g. go out on your own, earn a living and raise children). Bar Mitzvah is simply the age when a person is held responsible for his actions and minimally qualified to marry.
